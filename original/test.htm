Pinball table components

Move the mouse over a component to see its name. Click on a component to learn about its function.

Center post
The center post is a peg that pops up between the two flippers and helps to keep the ball from draining. You can raise the center post by hitting the hyperspace kickout three times. It will stay raised for 60 seconds before sinking back into the table.
Plunger
When you launch the ball, it must reach at least the first deployment light to enter the table. When it reaches this light, the reflex shot lights are turned on. If the ball drains within the first 15 seconds, you get to replay the ball.
Progress lights
At the advanced level, you complete missions and receive promotions to move up in rank from Cadet to Fleet Admiral. Your progress is tracked by the progress lights. When you complete a mission, one or more progress lights are turned on. When all the progress lights are on, you move up in rank.
Rank lights
Your current rank, from Cadet to Fleet Admiral, is tracked by the rank lights. The types of missions you must complete depend on your current rank.
Booster targets
The bank of booster targets is made up of drop targets. For each target you drop, you receive points. When you drop all three targets, you receive additional points, the first booster light turns on, and the first play mode is activated. This process repeats itself for each time you drop all three booster targets until all four booster lights are turned on.
Space warp rollover
If the ball moves over the space warp rollover light, you receive 10,000 points, and the return lane lights are turned on.
Deployment lights
A skill shot is made by launching the ball only partway up the deployment chute and letting it fall back down the chute and onto the main table. Points are awarded based on how many deployment lights are lit.
Medal targets
The bank of medal targets is made up of drop targets. When you drop a medal target, you earn points. When you drop all three medal targets, you are awarded an extra ball and the blue medal light is turned on. This process repeats itself for each time all three medal targets are dropped until the purple medal light is turned on. If either the blue or red medal light is turned on when you drop a medal target, you earn extra points. If the purple medal light is turned on when you drop a medal target, you are awarded an extra ball.
Space warp target
The space warp target is a spot target. When you hit this target, you receive points and the space warp target light is turned on. In addition, the wormhole destination lights are turned on and the wormholes are activated.
Attack bumpers
The status of these four large Attack Bumpers is upgraded one color (from blue to green, then to yellow, and then to red) each time all three re-entry lights are turned on. The higher the attack bumper status, the greater the number of points awarded when the bumpers are hit. After 60 seconds, the attack bumper status is degraded.
Hyperspace lights
When the ball is ejected from the hyperspace chute, one more hyperspace light is turned on. Each additional light rewards more points. Hyperspace lights are reset after 60 seconds.
Hazard target bank
The left and right banks of hazard targets are each made up of three spot targets. When you hit a spot target in the bank of hazard targets, you receive points. If all three left hazard lights are on, the left kicker is activated. If all three right hazard lights are on, the right kicker is activated.
Hyperspace kickout
The hyperspace chute curves around the upper-right end of the table. The hyperspace lights are located over the chute. At the end of the chute is the hyperspace kickout. If the ball lands in the hyperspace kickout, points are awarded depending on how many hyperspace lights are on, and the ball is ejected and sent back down the hyperspace chute. Hyperspace lights are reset after 60 seconds.
Re-entry lanes
When the ball rolls through one of the three re-entry lanes, you receive points and the re-entry light above the lane is turned on or off. If all three re-entry lights are on, the attack bumpers are upgraded.
Flipper
Besides hitting the ball, each time you move a flipper, the re-entry lights and launch lights are rotated clockwise if you move the right flipper, and counter-clockwise if you move the left flipper.
Replay light
The replay light indicates that you get to replay the ball. When this happens, nothing on the table resets or changes, and you do not receive the bonus. You get only one replay at a time.
Rebound
There are four rebounds on the table. Each time you hit a rebound, you receive points.
Kicker
When the ball hits a kicker, it is ejected up the lane and back into play. You can open the kicker gates by hitting all the hazard targets (the right hazard targets for the right kicker, the left hazard targets for the left kicker).
Return lane
The return lanes return the ball to the flippers. When the ball rolls down one of these lanes, you receive points. If the return lane light above the lane is on, the number of points is doubled.
Bonus lane
When the ball rolls down the bonus lane, you receive points and your fuel supply is increased. If the bonus light is on, you receive bonus points.
Out lane
The two outer lanes where the ball can potentially drain behind the flippers if the kicker gate is not open. If the extra ball light above the lane is on, you receive an extra ball.
Extra ball light
Indicates that an extra ball will be awarded of the ball drains down the out lane.
Tilt light
You can nudge the table to the left, to the right, and upwards with the keyboard controls. If you nudge the table too much, it will tilt. When the table tilts, the tilt light goes on and all components stop working until the ball drains. The table is reset with the next ball.
Mission targets
When you hit a mission target, or a combination of mission targets, you are notified of the mission that is associated with that target or combination of targets. Blue arrows will then begin flashing on the table. The flashing arrows direct you to targets you must hit to complete the mission.
Launch bumpers
The status of these three small bumpers is upgraded one color (from blue to green, then to yellow, and then to red) each time all three launch lane lights are turned on. The higher the launch bumper status, the greater the number of points awarded when the bumpers are hit. After 60 seconds, the launch bumper status is degraded.
Reflex shot light
When a ball reaches at least the first deployment light on the launch ramp, the reflex shot lights are turned on. You get 25,000 points for a reflex shot when the ball has just exited the launch ramp or hyperspace chute and you execute a quick return shot back to the same location. The reflex shot light in the hyperspace chute is set each time the ball lands in the hyperspace kickout. The reflex shot light for the launch ramp is set each time the ball drains from the ramp and it must be on to obtain the points. The reflex shot light goes off after five seconds.
Launch lanes
When the ball rolls through one of the three launch lanes, you receive points and the launch light above the lane is turned on or off. If all three launch lights are on, the launch bumpers are upgraded.
Fuel lights
The amount of fuel left in your ship is shown by the number of fuel lights turned on in the fuel chute. You can light fuel lights by rolling the ball over them, by sending the ball down the bonus lane, or by hitting all the fuel targets.
Space warp target
The space warp target is a spot target. When you hit this target, you receive points and the space warp target light is turned on. In addition, the wormhole destination lights are turned on and the wormholes are activated.
Black hole knockout
This is located just under the ramp. If the ball lands here, you receive 5,000 points.
Launch ramp
The launch ramp curves out toward the left edge of the table and contains the three launch lanes and three launch bumpers. There are three lights at the entrance to the ramp: the launch ramp reflex light, the time warp forward light, and the mission acceptance light. If any of these lights are turned on, you receive an award. When the ball drains from the launch ramp, the launch ramp reflex light is turned on.
Flag
Flags are flat, square objects that rotate on their axis when hit by the ball. There are two flags located on the Space Cadet 3D Pinball table: one in the fuel chute and one in the hyperspace chute. If a flag is rotated when the flag light is on, the score is five times greater. The color status of the wormhole destination lights is also upgraded when a flag is turned.
Wormhole
There are three wormholes on the table: green, red, and yellow. When a ball goes into any of the wormholes, it may come out of the same wormhole or out of a different wormhole, depending on which of the wormhole destination lights are on.
Field multiplier target
The bank of field multiplier targets is made up of drop targets. When you drop a field multiplier target, you receive points. When you drop all three targets, the first field multiplier light is turned on and you receive bonus points. This process repeats itself for each time you drop all three field multiplier targets until all four field multiplier lights are turned on. As each additional multiplier light is turned on, all points scored on the table are multiplied by a greater amount.
Fuel targets
The bank of fuel targets is made up of three spot targets. When you hit a fuel target, you receive points. If all three fuel target lights are on, your fuel supply is increased.
